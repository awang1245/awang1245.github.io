Black Tea

红茶 [hóng chá]

Black tea is fully oxidized, resulting in a deep color and full-bodied flavor. In China, where it originated, this style is actually called hóngchá (红茶), meaning red tea, referring to the amber-red color of the brew. Black tea later spread globally, especially to India and Sri Lanka, and is enjoyed plain or with milk depending on style and tradition.

Flavors can range from malty and robust (Assam), to muscatel and floral (Darjeeling), to smooth and cocoa-like (Chinese hongcha), or bright and citrusy (Ceylon). The oxidation process creates a rich, well-rounded taste with natural sweetness and gentle tannins. A good black tea should feel strong yet smooth, without harsh bitterness.
