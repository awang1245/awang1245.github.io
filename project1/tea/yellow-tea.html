Yellow Tea

黄茶 [huáng chá]

Yellow tea is rare and traditionally made in China. After a short heating step, the leaves undergo a “smothering” phase that slows oxidation slightly. This process smooths out grassy notes, creating a mellow cup.

Yellow tea tastes soft, lightly sweet, and mild, with gentle floral and nutty hints. It has a rounder, smoother flavor than green tea without bitterness. Because production is limited, it’s considered a special tea among enthusiasts.
