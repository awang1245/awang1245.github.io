White Tea

白茶 [bái chá]

White tea is the most delicate style of Camellia sinensis, processed with as little handling as possible to keep its natural sweetness. It originated in Fujian, China, made from early spring buds and young leaves covered in soft silver hairs. Known for its light flavor and gentle energy, white tea brews into a soft, smooth cup.

The leaves are picked and slowly withered for 1-2 days, allowing slight natural oxidation that builds sweetness without bitterness. Expect subtle notes of honey, fresh hay, mild fruit, and melon. Common types include Silver Needle (bud-only, very light) and White Peony (bud and leaves, slightly fuller).
