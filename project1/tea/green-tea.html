Green Tea

绿茶 [lǜ chá]

Green tea is heated soon after picking to stop oxidation, keeping its fresh, grassy flavor and bright green liquor. It is widely produced in China and Japan, each with distinct steaming or pan-firing styles. Green tea is known for clean, refreshing taste and a clear, focused feeling.

Flavors range from sea-sweet and umami (Japan) to nutty, vegetal, and lightly floral (China). Green tea is sensitive to hot water and can taste bitter if overheated. Good green tea has gentle sweetness and a smooth finish.
