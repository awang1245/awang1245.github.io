TEA CATALOG HOW TO PREPARE WHAT IS TEA

Green Tea

Fresh, grassy, and bright. Quickly heated after harvest to stop oxidation, preserving its vivid color and delicate flavor.

Longjing, Biluochun

绿茶

White Tea

Subtle, sweet, and delicate. Picked young and simply air-dried, resulting in minimally processed leaves with slight oxidation.

Silver Needle, White Peony

白茶

Yellow Tea

Smooth and mellow with a hint of fruitiness. “Sweltering” gently oxidizes the leaves, giving them a golden hue.

Junshan Yinzhen, Huoshan Huangya

黄茶

Oolong Tea

Complex flavor, ranging from floral and light to rich and toasted. Falls in between green and black tea with a semi-oxidized process.

Tieguanyin, Da Hong Pao

乌龙茶

Black Tea

Bold, malty, and full-bodied. Oxidized to allow the leaves to develop a darker color and robust flavor.

Keemun, Lapsang Souchong

红茶

Pu'er Tea

Earthy, smooth, and aged. Undergoes microbial fermentation and post-oxidation, maturing over time to add depth.

Sheng Pu'er, Shou Pu'er
